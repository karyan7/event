About the Festival

The Indian Classical Music & Dance Festival is an annual event celebrating the rich heritage of Indian classical arts. The festival has a long history of bringing together the best maestros of Indian classical music and dance, offering a platform to preserve and promote these ancient art forms. Featured Artists

Pandit Ravi Shankar

Pandit Ravi Shankar was a legendary sitar virtuoso and a composer who is known for his profound influence on the world of Indian classical music. A global ambassador for the art, he has played a key role in introducing Indian music to the West, collaborating with musicians like George Harrison of The Beatles. His contributions have inspired generations of musicians and have earned him numerous accolades, including several Grammy Awards.

Ustad Zakir Hussain

Ustad Zakir Hussain is a master tabla player and a world-renowned percussionist. Born into a family of musical prodigies, he has performed with a wide range of artists from different genres, bridging the gap between Indian classical music and global music traditions. His precision, creativity, and dynamic stage presence have made him a favorite among audiences worldwide.

Dr. Sonal Mansingh

Dr. Sonal Mansingh is a celebrated Indian classical dancer known for her mastery in Odissi and Bharatanatyam. A recipient of the Padma Vibhushan, she has dedicated her life to the preservation and promotion of Indian classical dance forms. Her performances are characterized by deep emotional expression, technical prowess, and a profound connection to Indian mythology and literature.

Shri Birju Maharaj

Shri Birju Maharaj was a legendary Kathak dancer and one of the most influential figures in Indian classical dance. Hailing from a lineage of distinguished dancers, he brought Kathak to new heights through his innovative choreography and storytelling. His graceful movements, intricate footwork, and emotive expressions have left an indelible mark on the art form.
